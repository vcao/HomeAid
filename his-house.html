      Four to six months for adults to secure emplyoment and save money for permanent housing

    H.I.S. House transitional shelter was founded in 1989 by Placentia Presbyterian Church. Since its inception, H.I.S. House has been home to over 2000 families, singles and children.

    Once a three bedroom two bath farmhouse, H.I.S. House has undergone two major expansion remodels and now consists of 13 guest rooms, five restrooms, library, two kitchens, two family rooms, a living room and resident manager quarters. Each family lives in a private guest room, singles share a room with another single. During the four to six month program adults secure employment and save money for permanent housing.

    Success Stories

    Meet Travis

    Meet Travis, a graduate of H.I.S. House in Placentia. When his wife suddenly passed away, Travis became a single father of two young children and his life began to rapidly spin out of control. Travis lost his job, his home and then his children before hitting rock bottom. Then he discovered H.I.S. House, a transitional shelter that helped him to regain self-sufficiency by providing weekly life skill classes, counseling, and finance classes. Within 30 days of entering the program, Travis became employed again. In less than 80 days, Travis got his children back and within a few months, Travis had graduated from the program and was living a life of self-sufficiency again.

    HomeAid first renovated H.I.S. House in 1992 and returned in 2012 to provide an Environmental Sustainability project on the existing two-story farmhouse. Upgrades included: replacing both HVAC systems, light replacements with new energy efficient fluorescents, attic insulation, Energy Star Roofing and Solar and Thermal Panel Systems and the addition of Energy Star appliances. Retrofitting H.I.S. House with green, energy efficient upgrades will enable the shelter to reduce operating costs for energy, lowering monthly expenses for electricity, gas, and water resulting in increased comfort and efficiencies and an overall reduction in the structure’s environmental impact.

    What HomeAid Did

    HomeAid’s role as the developer for H.I.S. House was to bring together the resources needed to retrofit an existing facility to modernize it and make it energy efficient.

    HomeAid Orange County’s 49th project is a century-old farmhouse in Placentia that was converted into a shelter for the temporarily homeless and is now hailed as a model of environmental sustainability. Founded by Placentia Presbyterian Church, the charming two-story farmhouse was recently completely retrofitted by HomeAid Orange County with energy-efficient upgrades as part of a national Environmental Sustainability Program (ESP) created by HomeAid America and Walmart and supported by Owens Corning.

    The target metrics for the national ESP program are to save at least 40 percent in annual utility costs and to recover the cost of the energy saving retrofits in five years or less. Currently, H.I.S. House has yearly energy costs of $10,364. As a result of the complete energy retrofit, estimated savings are $8,950 – an 86 percent annual reduction in energy costs.

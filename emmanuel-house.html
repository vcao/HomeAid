    Mercy House operates Emmanuel House to serve homeless individuals suffering from HIV/AIDS. Homeless individuals suffering from HIV/AIDs are oftenly ostracized from communities because of a misperception that the diseases are easily transmitted. Therefore, individuals have no other place to go. Mercy House requires sobriety and coaching toward rebuilding productive and healthy living opportunities.

    Success Stories

    Jesse's Story

    Jesse, a carpenter, mechanic and self-proclaimed jack of all trades, worked hard all his life until one day his boss introduced him to Meth. He ended up sick with meningitis and HIV/AIDs. No longer able to work, he ended up homeless living under bridges and sleeping on benches. He was very near death weighing only 121 pounds and a T-cell count of only 12 (normal for a healthy adult is a T-cell count between 500-1000). Then he discovered Emmanuel House a transitional shelter for homeless single adults living with HIV/Aids that focuses on improving health, material and financial self-sufficiency, and life skills.

    Jesse, no longer on the brink of death, now weighs 186 pounds and has a T-cell count of 141. He is also 20 months sober, volunteers and has commitments. When he discovered Emmanuel House, it opened a doorway of safe passage he needed to survive.

    Emmanuel House is operated by Mercy House and provides transitional housing to residents for 6-24 months depending on the individual case management plan. Every resident that lives in Mercy House shelters adheres to specific programs that promote their welfare. Each homeless individual that Mercy House serves receives direct personal care through their Planning for Success Program, Wellness Program, and Learning Matters Program.

    What HomeAid Did

    HomeAid's role as the developer of Emmanuel House was to work directly with the builder and service provider until this project was completed. This involved each phase of development from land acquisition, to funding, to design, to entitlements, to construction, to interior design and landscaping.

    Trade Partners & Donors List

    HomeAid Orange County's 32nd project and California's first new shelter devoted specifically to homeless adults living with HIV or AIDS, Emmanuel House is the direct result of the cooperation of over 200 businesses and individuals.

    Emmanuel House, operated by Mercy House, began as a 6,000-square-foot one-story home designed by RNM Architects, took on a life of its own and ultimately evolved into the 16,000 square-foot, two-story facility that now stands in the heart of Santa Ana's historic French Park district.

    Through HomeAid's efforts, 70% of the completed home was donated, amounting to a value of $3,376,676 in contributions to the over-all cost of a $4.2 million project. Special recognition goes to Builder Captain, John Laing Homes for donating $1 million to the shelter venture as well as Creative Design Concepts for donating $300,000 of interior design components.

    Since 1989, HomeAid Orange County has ended homelessness for thousands of families.

    HomeAid Orange County is a 501 (c) 3 charitable non-profit organization that builds and renovates shelters for those in Orange County who find themselves homeless due to sudden job loss, catastrophic illness, spousal desertion, and domestic violence.

    Our mission is to end homelessness.

    About HomeAid

    Our Mission

    HomeAid’s vision is to end homelessness through advocacy, development and service. Charged with the mission to build new lives for homeless families and individuals through housing and community outreach, HomeAid has been working to solve the social crisis of homelessness for 25 years.

    Many people believe that the homeless are on the street due to choice, but the real truth is most are homeless due to circumstances such as job loss, illness, abandonment, abuse or the financial inability to make ends meet.

    Over 12,700 people find themselves homeless in Orange County every year and unbelievably, 20% of those are children. In the past 25 years, 50 shelters have been developed that have housed more than 50,000 homeless men, women and children. Join us to end this issue and help HomeAid develop shelter, reach into the community in service and advocate for those who experience homelessness.
